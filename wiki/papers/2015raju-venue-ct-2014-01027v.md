## Summary

The work develops a **ReaxFF description for Li–C interactions** (trained against **vdW-corrected DFT**) and apply it to **graphitic and nanostructured carbon anodes** for Li-ion storage. **Grand canonical Monte Carlo** together with reactive sampling is used to study **Li intercalation stages**, in-plane ordering, and interlayer spacing in graphite, including **vacancy-bearing “defective graphite”** where increased Li/C and voltage shifts mirror trends associated with **Li plating** in recent experiments. Additional geometries (for example **onion-like and nanorod-like** carbon models) illustrate how **dimensionality and defects** change rate-capable Li populations at the atomistic level.

## Methods

- **DFT-derived training** for Li–C ReaxFF terms (vdW-corrected references as described in the article).
- **GCMC + reactive MD workflows** to impose Li chemical potential and relax local coordination in large cells.

## Findings

- The new Li/C ReaxFF reproduces **intercalation voltage trends** and **staging-related structural signatures** for stage I/II graphite in line with experiment and selected DFT benchmarks.
- **Vacancy defects** increase effective capacity signatures (Li/C) and shift voltages in a direction consistent with **plating-prone** experimental scenarios.
- **0D defective onion-like carbon** vs **1D nanorod** examples differentiate **fast surface adsorption** pathways from **edge-nucleated intercalation** that requires a critical Li density.

## Limitations

- **Electrolyte, SEI, and explicit electron transfer** at the anode are outside the classical reactive Hamiltonian used here.
- **Parameter transfer** to highly fluorinated, sulfurized, or strongly solvated environments is not automatic and needs separate validation.

## Relevance to group
