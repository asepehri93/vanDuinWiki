## Summary

This paper reports **ReaxFF parameterization and reactive MD** aimed at **gas-phase chemistry** relevant to **CVD-style growth** of **boron nitride nanostructures (BNNS)**. The force field targets BNNS formation from precursors such as **BN** vs **HBNH**, compares predicted BNNS quality and size, analyzes temperature effects (including loss of closed BNNS motifs to polymeric structures at very high \(T\)), and examines additives such as **H\(_2\)** that disrupt closed-shell BNNS formation. Results are discussed against prior DFTB/QCMD literature for gas-phase BN synthesis, emphasizing ReaxFF throughput advantages for large, reactive ensembles.

## Methods

ReaxFF reactive MD; gas-phase synthesis trajectories with BN vs HBNH precursors; scans over temperature/pressure and hydrogen-containing environments; comparison points to prior DFTB gas-phase BNNS work.

## Findings

**BN**-type precursors favor larger, higher-quality BNNS vs **HBNH** (smaller, flatter, lower-quality, less temperature-sensitive in the sense described). **Closed** BNNS motifs from BN precursors give way to **complex polymeric structures** at **2500 K** and above. **H\(_2\)** strongly perturbs formation pathways by **suppressing closed BNNS** formation. Authors position ReaxFF as capturing key gas-phase BN chemistry trends for synthesis guidance.


## Limitations

Galley PDF; classical reactive model uncertainty for high-temperature radical-rich chemistry; direct quantitative linkage to specific experimental CVD recipes still requires targeted validation.
