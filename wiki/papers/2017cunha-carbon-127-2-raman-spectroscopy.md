## Summary

**Low-temperature Raman** (**20 K**) probes **Ar** and **Xe** **physisorption** on **SWCNT bundles**, revealing **blueshifts** in **RBM**, **G**, and **2D** bands attributed to **solidified gas** in **groove** and **external** sites imparting **hydrostatic-like compression**. **ReaxFF modeling** accompanies experiment to interpret **interactions** as weak, **non-doping** **physisorption** (linewidth/intensity largely unchanged). **van Duin** and **Yoon** contribute **computational** support within a broad **PSU**/international collaboration.

## Methods

- **Raman spectroscopy** on **bundle** samples with **noble gas** dosing at **cryogenic** temperature.
- **ReaxFF** simulations supporting **adsorption-site** and **stress** interpretations.

## Findings

- **Ar** vs **Xe** produce similar **frequency shifts**, implying comparable **bundle–gas** interaction strength in the studied regime.
- **~4 cm\(^{-1}\)** **RBM** shifts cited as evidence of **adsorption-induced** compression effects.

## Limitations

- **Heterogeneous** tube diameters in bundles complicate site-by-site assignment from **optical** averages alone.
