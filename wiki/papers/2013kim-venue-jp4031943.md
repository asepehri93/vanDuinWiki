## One-paragraph summary

The authors develop a **ReaxFF parameterization for Ti/O/Cl/H** aimed at **plasma-relevant etching** chemistry: **Ti metal** and **TiO₂** slabs exposed to **Cl₂** and **HCl**. The **Ti–Cl** parameters are fit to a **QM training set** including **dissociation curves**, angular/dihedral scans, **Ti + chlorine gas reactions**, and **TiClₓ crystal heats of formation**, then merged with an existing **Ti–O–H** description. MD etching simulations compare **HCl vs Cl₂** etching ratios to **experiment** with **satisfactory agreement** per abstract.

## Methods

- **ReaxFF** energy terms as standard partitioning (bond/over/under/lone pair/valence/Coulomb/vdW with EEM charges).
- **Successive one-parameter search** for Ti–Cl bonds and selected angles (Ti–Cl–Ti, Cl–Ti–Cl, etc.) against QM.

## Findings

- Reports good reproduction of QM training metrics for small clusters and **TiClₓ** crystals.
- Demonstrates **atomistic etching** trajectories with qualitative experimental alignment on relative etchant performance.

## Limitations

- Plasma chemistry involves excited species not fully captured by ground-state FF training; transferability to fluorine/halo mixtures not established here.

## Relevance to group

Shows **ReaxFF extension** to **Ti halide** chemistry for **semiconductor processing** adjacent to oxide interface studies.
