## Summary

**ReaxFF sliding simulations** probe **single-asperity tribochemistry** of **amorphous silica** in **water** and in **phosphoric acid solution** under varied **normal loads**. The study report that **water can suppress strong adhesion** between a-SiO2 surfaces at **low load**, while **higher load** promotes **interfacial siloxane bond formation and rupture**. In **phosphoric acid**, **acid polymerization** yields **oligomers** at milder contact pressures, whereas **higher load** drives **degradative tribochemical reactions** that increase wear. **Bridging oxygens** are highlighted as key to **covalent bridge formation**, with **hydrogen** weakening bridges and enabling **shear-driven rupture**.

## Methods

- **Reactive MD (ReaxFF)** of **a-SiO2 / a-SiO2** contacts with explicit **H2O** or **H3PO4** environments.
- **Normal pressure** sweeps with lateral sliding to accumulate tribochemical statistics.

The article further notes that reactive molecular dynamics (ReaxFF) simulations are used to explore the atomic-level tribochemical mechanism of amorphous silica (a-SiO 2)i n a nanoscale, single-asperity contact in an aqueous environment. These sliding simulations are performed in both a phosphoric acid solution and in pure water under di ﬀerent normal pressures. Water molecules could help avoid direct adhesive interaction between a-SiO 2 surfaces in pure water under low normal load. However, formation and rupture of interfacial siloxane bonds are obviously observed under higher normal load.

## Findings

- **Load-dependent** transition from **passivation / low adhesion** toward **covalent bridging** in pure water.
- **Phosphoric acid** chemistry adds **polymerization** and **accelerated tribochemical wear** at higher loads in the scenarios summarized in the abstract.
- **Hydrogen** assists **bridge scission** under shear, modulating **wear vs. lubrication** outcomes.


From the abstract: the results show that tribochemical processes have profound consequences on tribological performance. The bridging oxygen atoms in silica play an important role in the formation of interfacial covalent bonds, and hydrogen is found to have a weakening e ﬀect on these bonds, resulting in the rupture during shear-related loading. In phosphoric acid solution, polymerization of phosphoric acid molecules occurs, yielding oligomers under lower load, and tribochemical reactions between the molecules and the sliding surfaces could enhance wear under higher load. This work sheds light on tribochemical reactions as a mechanism for lubrication and wear in water-based or other tribological systems.

## Limitations

- **Single-asperity** models omit **multi-contact statistics** and **third-body particles** from wear debris.
- **Force-field** limits on **pH**, **ionic strength**, and **long-range polarization** may affect absolute reaction rates.
