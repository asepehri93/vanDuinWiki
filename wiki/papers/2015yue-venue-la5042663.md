## Summary

**ReaxFF sliding simulations** probe **single-asperity tribochemistry** of **amorphous silica** in **water** and in **phosphoric acid solution** under varied **normal loads**. The study report that **water can suppress strong adhesion** between a-SiO2 surfaces at **low load**, while **higher load** promotes **interfacial siloxane bond formation and rupture**. In **phosphoric acid**, **acid polymerization** yields **oligomers** at milder contact pressures, whereas **higher load** drives **degradative tribochemical reactions** that increase wear. **Bridging oxygens** are highlighted as key to **covalent bridge formation**, with **hydrogen** weakening bridges and enabling **shear-driven rupture**.

## Methods

- **Reactive MD (ReaxFF)** of **a-SiO2 / a-SiO2** contacts with explicit **H2O** or **H3PO4** environments.
- **Normal pressure** sweeps with lateral sliding to accumulate tribochemical statistics.

## Findings

- **Load-dependent** transition from **passivation / low adhesion** toward **covalent bridging** in pure water.
- **Phosphoric acid** chemistry adds **polymerization** and **accelerated tribochemical wear** at higher loads in the scenarios summarized in the abstract.
- **Hydrogen** assists **bridge scission** under shear, modulating **wear vs. lubrication** outcomes.

## Limitations

- **Single-asperity** models omit **multi-contact statistics** and **third-body particles** from wear debris.
- **Force-field** limits on **pH**, **ionic strength**, and **long-range polarization** may affect absolute reaction rates.
